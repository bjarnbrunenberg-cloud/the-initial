Research Context

Strategic Foundation — Evidence-Based Experimentation

This comprehensive strategy document synthesises data from six research methods conducted across Semrush’s five free tools pages: heatmap and scroll analysis using Clarity, 150+ session recordings analysed with behavioural coding, direct user interviews with five distinct personas, customer support ticket analysis, 5-second rapid testing, and structured heuristic review. The research reveals three dominant patterns: competing CTAs fragmenting attention above the fold, results pages failing to guide high-intent users toward paid conversion, and a missing value bridge between free satisfaction and premium pricing (€4.66–€139.95/month).

The three pillars below address these patterns through structured experimentation: first, reducing cognitive load and friction in the free-to-paid journey; second, building trust through data transparency and differentiation from competing tools like Google Analytics; and third, matching CTA messaging and experience to five distinct user personas. The Big Swings target architectural changes; the 12 A/B tests nest within the three pillars, prioritised by estimated impact and implementation effort.

Big Swings

Structural Changes to the Free-to-Paid Journey

These three experiments target the architecture of the free-to-paid conversion journey itself — not individual interface elements, but fundamental structural decisions that shape the entire experience. Each was the highest-voted idea in the collaborative heuristic session and addresses a root cause, not a symptom. Implementation effort is higher, but impact spans the entire user journey.
3 experiment ideas
1

Email-Gated Results Unlock

All Free Tool Results Pages

CURRENT STATE

Query 1: Unlimited access, no capture
Query 2: Full results shown
Friction: 34–46% zero-click rate — users consume and leave

PROPOSED CHANGE

Query 1: Ungated, full results
Query 2: Email overlay: “Save your results & unlock unlimited searches”
Outcome: Lightweight capture, follow-up sequence to trial
Observation

Session recordings show 34–46% zero-click rate on Website Traffic Checker and Keyword Magic. Users consume top-level results and leave. No mechanism captures users or moves them down-funnel. Free tool completely satisfies the need with no bridge to paid.

Idea

Gate the second or third query behind lightweight email capture (no credit card, no account creation). After first ungated result, trigger overlay: “Save your results & get unlimited searches — enter your email.” Follow-up email sequence introduces paid features.

Hypothesis

Because session recordings show that users consume top-level results and leave without converting (34–46% zero-click rate), we believe that gating the second or third query behind an email capture will increase lead capture rate for free tool users, measured by email submissions per session and downstream trial starts within 7 days.

2

AI Transparency Seal on Data-Sensitive Tools

Website Traffic Checker • Backlinks • Site Audit Results

CURRENT STATE

Metric card: Authority Score: 42
No context: No explanation of calculation
User doubt: “Is this all AI? How do I know this is real?”

PROPOSED CHANGE

Metric card: Authority Score: 42 🔍 How we calculate
On click: Tooltip: “Based on 2.3M clickstream data points & SERP modelling”
Outcome: Trust signal visible at point of scepticism
Observation

User interviews revealed deep scepticism: “Is this all AI that’s doing the information gathering?” Website Traffic Checker triggers “this is like GA” comparisons. No trust signal exists at the top of results pages where 80%+ attention concentrates.

Idea

Add visible “How we calculate this” badge next to key metrics. Clicking expands 2-sentence explanation. On Website Traffic Checker: “This is not Google Analytics data — it’s an independent estimate based on clickstream panels and SERP modelling.”

Hypothesis

Because user interviews show that data scepticism causes users to dismiss results and abandon the tool, we believe that adding a visible data methodology explanation next to key metrics will increase results page engagement and downstream trial starts, measured by scroll depth on results pages and trial conversion rate.

Rank Checker → Cross-Tool Action Pipeline

Rank Checker Results Page

CURRENT STATE

Results: Ranking table with keyword data
Next step: User must navigate elsewhere manually
Drop-off: Only 5–23% visit a second page

PROPOSED CHANGE

Results: Same ranking table
Below table: “What to do next” section with 3 contextual cards
Outcome: Pre-filled cross-tool access with user’s domain
Observation

Rank Checker has the deepest engagement: 8.3 min average session, 14.2 clicks, 22.9% visiting 2+ pages, 0% zero-click rate. High-intent Verifier persona investigating SEO position. No structured path from “I checked rankings” to “Here’s what to do about it.”

Idea

After rankings table, add “What to do next” section with 3 contextual cards based on user’s results: (1) “3 keywords dropped — run a Site Audit”; (2) “Competitor outranks you on 7 keywords — see their backlinks”; (3) “4 keywords on page 2 — use Keyword Magic for opportunities.”

Hypothesis

Because the Rank Checker attracts high-intent users with the deepest engagement (8.3 min, 14.2 clicks, 0% zero-click), we believe that adding contextual cross-tool recommendations after results will increase multi-tool usage and trial starts, measured by cross-tool click-through rate and trial conversion within session.

Pillar 1

Reduce Cognitive Load & Friction in Results Discovery

Four A/B tests targeting above-fold experience, results navigation, and exit friction. These experiments address the observation that users engage in short bursts (30–60 seconds) and abandon results pages quickly due to competing CTAs, dense layouts, and missing save mechanisms.
4 experiment ideas
1

Mobile Results Redesign — Collapsed Sections

Backlinks • Website Traffic Checker — Mobile

Observation

Mobile users have shallower scroll depth. Results pages designed desktop-first. On Backlinks, 50% leave after first 5 results. Dense tables not optimised for small screens.

Idea

Collapse results into expandable summary cards (Authority Score: 42 | Backlinks: 1,234). Each expands on tap. “Unlock full report” pinned at bottom drives conversion.

Hypothesis

Because mobile users show shallower scroll depth and higher drop-off on results pages, we believe that collapsing results into expandable sections will increase mobile engagement and sign-up CTR, measured by results page scroll depth on mobile and sticky CTA clicks on mobile.

2

Exit-Intent “Save Your Report” Overlay

All Results Pages
Your Report Is Ready
Save it to your free account & revisit anytime
Observation

“Tab-Switcher Syndrome”: users run query, glance 30–60 seconds, switch tabs. Tab stays open 4–11 minutes with zero interaction. No save mechanism or reminder.

Idea

On cursor-to-tab-bar (desktop) or back-button (mobile), trigger lightweight overlay: “Your report is ready — save it to your free account.” Single email field. No pricing, no upsell. Dismissible with “Maybe later.”

Hypothesis

Because session recordings show users consume results in 30–60 second bursts and abandon tabs, we believe that triggering a “Save this report” overlay on exit intent will increase email capture and return visits, measured by overlay submission rate and 7-day return rate.

3

Remove “How to Use” Section from Tool Pages

All 5 Free Tool Landing Pages

CURRENT STATE

Above fold: Hero + CTA
Below fold: “How to use” section with 4 steps
Heatmap data: Sharp drop-off, near-zero engagement

PROPOSED CHANGE

Above fold: Hero + CTA (same)
Below fold: Social proof strip: “2M+ searches run this month • Trusted by 5K+ companies”
Outcome: Reduced page length, increased CTA visibility
Observation

Heatmaps across all 5 tools show sharp drop-off at “How to use” section. Attention heatmaps confirm near-zero focus. Users go directly to input bar and start searching.

Idea

Remove section entirely. Replace with compact social proof strip: “[X] million searches run this month • Trusted by [Y] companies • No sign-up required.”

Hypothesis

Because heatmaps show near-zero engagement with “How to use” sections across all 5 tools, we believe that removing this section will reduce page length and increase visibility of conversion elements, measured by scroll depth and sign-up CTA visibility rate.

Pillar 2

Build Trust & Transparency on Data Accuracy & Scope

Four A/B tests targeting data credibility and trust signals. These experiments address user scepticism (“Is this AI? How do I know this is real?”) and positioning differentiation from Google Analytics by surfacing methodology and pricing context.
4 experiment ideas
1

GA Differentiation Statement on Website Traffic Checker

semrush.com/website/ — Landing & Results
semrush.com/website-traffic-analyzer

Website Traffic Checker

See estimated traffic for any website — including your competitors
Unlike Google Analytics, see estimated traffic for any website — including your competitors
Observation

Status Quo Bias: users’ first reaction is “this is like GA,” making tool feel redundant. Tool doesn’t explain it provides competitor traffic data (GA can’t), estimated breakdowns for sites you don’t own, industry benchmarking.

Idea

Below search bar, add one-liner: “Unlike Google Analytics, see estimated traffic for any website — including your competitors.” On results page: “Competitor intelligence • Data GA can’t show you.”

Hypothesis

Because user interviews show the Website Traffic Checker triggers “this is like GA” comparisons, we believe that adding a visible differentiation statement will increase tool engagement and results page depth, measured by second-query rate and scroll depth.

Pillar 3

Match User Intent Across Tool Personas & Reduce CTA Ambiguity

Four A/B tests targeting messaging clarity and persona-specific CTAs. These experiments resolve the contradiction between “No sign-up required” and “Sign Up” and enable high-intent users (Rank Checker, Site Audit) to discover adjacent tools within their engagement window.
4 experiment ideas
1

Dynamic CTA Copy by Tool Persona

All 5 Free Tool Pages
CTA VARIANTS BY TOOL
Website Traffic Checker
Keyword Magic
Rank Checker
Site Audit
Observation

5 personas with engagement from 1.9 to 11 minutes. Generic “Try Semrush”/“Sign Up” cannot match different intents. Website: 0.05% CTR; Rank Checker: 0.06% CTR.

Idea

Tool-specific CTA copy: Website → “See who visits your competitors”; Keyword Magic → “Unlock 26.9B keywords”; Rank Checker → “Track your rankings daily”; Site Audit → “Get your SEO action plan.”

Hypothesis

Because 5 distinct personas show engagement ranging from 1.9 to 11 minutes, we believe that tailoring CTA copy to each tool’s user intent will increase CTA CTR per tool page, measured by tool-specific CTA CTR (baseline: 0.05–0.06%).

2

Resolve “No Sign-Up” vs. “Sign Up” Contradiction

All 5 Free Tool Pages
Hero Message
No sign-up required to get started
VARIANT A
VARIANT B
Both resolve semantic contradiction while driving account creation
Observation

5-second testing shows user confusion between hero “No sign-up required” and sticky “Sign Up.” One user: “I didn’t even notice that part.” Messaging promises no commitment; CTA demands it.

Idea

Change sticky CTA to “Create Free Account” (Variant A) or “Create a FREE Account” (Variant B). Both resolve semantic contradiction while still driving account creation. Test both variants.

Hypothesis

Because 5-second testing shows user confusion between “No sign-up required” and “Sign Up,” we believe that changing the CTA to “Create Free Account” will increase sticky CTA CTR, measured by sticky CTA click-through rate.
