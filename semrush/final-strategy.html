3-Month Experimentation Strategy Based on 6 Research Methods

This strategy synthesises findings from six research methods across Semrush’s five free tool pages: heatmap and scroll analysis (Microsoft Clarity, 12–19 March, 30,000+ sessions), 150+ session recordings per page with AI-assisted behavioural coding, 5 user interviews with SMB users (18–25 March), customer service analysis with product/CS representative, 5-second rapid impression testing (N=50 per page, US/UK/Canada), and a collaborative heuristic review with both Semrush and agency teams.

(1)

Free tool pages have 3+ competing CTAs that fragment attention. “Try Semrush” gets 0.05–0.16% clicks whilst tool CTAs get 12–18%.

(2)

Power users with muscle memory extract one data point in 30–60 seconds and leave. 80%+ never visit a second page.

(3)

No value bridge between free satisfaction and €139.95/month pricing. Users can’t justify the cost because they’ve never seen what “more” looks like.

Heuristic Analysis

Top 5 Learnings from the Collaborative Heuristic Review

01

“Try Semrush” pulls users out of their goal. They came for a keyword list and want to see results fast — not evaluate a product. Every element on the page should serve the tool, not compete with it.

02

Users don’t understand why Semrush is powerful. “How did it get to these keywords?” was a recurring question. There’s no methodology explanation, no data source transparency, no reason to trust the numbers over a free alternative like Google.

03

When users hit the pricing page, they don’t understand the value for their business. The jump from free tool to €139.95/month Pro or €249.95/month Guru feels disconnected — there’s no bridge that translates tool results into business impact before the ask.

04

There’s no mechanism to keep users engaged after the first query. The team voted for an engaging funnel: temptation content (tutorials, case studies, “how someone improved their business with these keywords”), a next free tool as a logical step, and content like blog posts or YouTube tutorials to keep users in the Semrush ecosystem.

05

No way to capture user data through utility. The team voted to add “send this list via email” or “add to Google Sheets” — share results when people share their email. A value exchange, not a gate.
